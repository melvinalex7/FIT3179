The World Happiness Report is a landmark survey of the state of global happiness. Several Hapiness Indicators are used to assess the overall hapiness of countries. The report is now gaining popularity more than ever as countries around the world now use happiness indicators to reform government services and guide policy decisions. Several leading experts from a variety of disciplines, including economics, psychology, survey analysis, national statistics, health, and public policy, analyse how different indicators contribute to a country's development.

Did you know?

The report was first published in 2012 and is currently published yearly by a group of independent experts working in collaboration with United Nations.

Key Happiness Indicators

There are 6 major factors that are used to assess a country's happiness

Economy

Influence of Average Earnings of a person (GDP) on overall happiness score.

Family

Support from Family and living conditions to raise a family has direct influence on happiness.

Health

Conditions that foster a healthy lifestyle and higher life expectancy contribute to happiness.

Freedom

Satisfaction of one's freedom to make life choices influences overall happiness.

Trust

Likeliness of governments being corrupt affects the trust and overall happiness of its citizens.

Generosity

Likeliness of Individuals contributing to charity will elevate the overall happiness of its residents.

Do Happier Countries Live Longer?

Evaluating the co-relation between the Happiness Score and Life Expectancy of a country

According to scientific studies, happier nations have longer life expectancy rates. This could be because to various factors, such as the fact that happier nations typically have higher levels of social cohesiveness and trust, which can result in better health outcomes. Aside from that, happy nations typically have greater economic development, which can increase access to better healthcare and other amenities that promote health and longevity. Also, happier nations tend to have more effective public health institutions and policies, which can also lead to improved health outcomes.

It is important to mention that happiness is only one of several elements that can influence life expectancy. In addition, factors like access to healthcare, education, and income are crucial.

A positive co-relation is of happiness scores and life expectancy is evident from the visualisation's trend line.
